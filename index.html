  • Welcome to Belgrade city!

    Belgrade (Beograd) is the capital of Serbia, and has a population of around 1.6 million. It is situated in South-Eastern Europe, on the Balkan Peninsula, at the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers.
    Thank you. Belgrade Sunset

    It is one of the oldest cities in Europe and has since ancient times been an important focal point for traffic, an intersection of the roads of Eastern and Western Europe.

  • The Capital

    Belgrade is the capital of Serbian culture, education, science and economy. As a result of its tumultuous history, Belgrade has for centuries been home to many nationalities, with Serbs of the Orthodox Christian religion making up the majority of the population (90%).

    Knez Mihailova

    Knez Mihailova Street is a pedestrian zone and shopping center - protected by law as one of the oldest and most valuable monumental complexes of the city, with a large number of representative buildings and urban houses.

    Kalemegdan

    High above the Sava and Danube confluence, on the rocky ridge which opens the view of Novi Beograd, Zemun and wide plains of Pannonia, there is the Belgrade Fortress with Kalemegdan, the former historical and urban center of Belgrade.

    The first urban settlement was built here in the 3rd century BC by the Celts. It was then conquered by the Romans in the 1st century BC and named Singidunum.

    In the city centre, beneath the church of Saint Mark’s is an actual cave.The Šalitrena cave was used in the Roman period to excavate a stone mostly used to make sarcophaguses.

    Belgrade has more than one hundred caves, canals, tunnels, passages tell the story of its connection to various empires and states that ruled here over the ages.

    The Roman well has its two spiral sets of 212 steps. This well is 62 metres deep, which is 10 metres deeper than Belgrade’s lowest point, and full of mysteries.
